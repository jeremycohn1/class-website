4/6 Reading Response to: "The Coming Age of Calm Technology"

The authors’ analysis of the evolution of technology and computation challenged the way I previously thought about calmness. I had previously attached calmness to things like meditation, where sound and stimulus are minimized to near zero. This reading proposes that a constant and fluctuating peripheral hum of information can foster serenity. While at first this felt confusing (why would extra stuff going on in my surroundings help me focus and/or reduce the chance that I feel stressed?), it started to make sense when I thought about it through my major’s academic lens. We learn in psychology that our brain alarms us when novel stimuli enter our environment, but tunes out continuous and non-threatening information, a process called habituation. Ubiquitous computing provides an opportunity for us to take note of new and important buzzes as they fade in and out, while allowing irrelevant information to remain as background fuzz. As technology becomes synonymous with environment, we ought to be able to handle its omnipresence, and perhaps a white-noise-esque design of computational innovation is the best choice.
