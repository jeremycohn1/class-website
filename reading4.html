2/4 Reading Response to: "My website is a shifting house next to a river of knowledge. What could yours be?"

1. What did you think about the essay? Did anything in particular resonate?

This essay breaks down the common constraints that we often set for ourselves when considering the concept of a website. We frequently experience websites through the sole purpose of commerce, spaces that prioritize profit over people, when a website can be an environment for us to connect, learn and grow.

2. Laurel proposes a website could be a river, a garden, a thrown rock. If you have a website, what could your website be?

I’d like to think of my first website as a stream. It’s small but it has a clear direction and has the opportunity to grow as it gathers resources in its surroundings. Perhaps one day it will connect to rivers, lakes, oceans.

3. What can you do with a website that you make yourself that you can’t do if someone else makes your website?

You can develop its identity in a way only you can, and you can manage it in the way you want. For example, if your website is a shelf, the wood has your tree markings, and you get to pick how books are stacked or organized, which books stay, and which ones get removed.
