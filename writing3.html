4/15 Writing Exercise- Prompt: When do you cherish the slow or hard way of doing something? Why?

I am pretty bad at cooking. But I also highly value the ability to craft delicious meals, for multiple reasons. First, it feels pretty amazing to have someone cook food for you. Second, it feels even more amazing to be the one cooking for someone else (as long as they enjoy it). So, if I’m going to cook, and have some time, I like to put in some effort. Since I’m kind of slow and bad at following instructions, it takes me a while to do everything, like cutting vegetables and preparing grains. But I’ve accepted this snail’s pace (for now), and am happy to experience the cooking process for longer. I can ensure my slices are even, that measurements are precise, and that I limit ingredient waste. And yeah, I’m sure most could do it with the same or better quality than me and also faster, and I’m okay with that.

Acknowledging this, I have added new aspects to my cooking to mask my grumbling stomach. I now bring a speaker down to the kitchen so I can listen to music when I cook. I experiment with new styles and vegetables to maximize my learning experience. That also provides me with an excellent excuse for my slowness or badness.

I think I am becoming better at cooking day by day. I’ve perfected my burrito bowls, and have moved on to more complex things, like fish. Even when I’m really good at cooking in the future, I may still be slow, if I just enjoy the process a lot. At least until I have kids, when I’ll probably have to pick up the pace.
