4/8 Writing Exercise- Prompt: What is a ritual, practice, or routine in your life that is important for your psychological wellbeing and/or fulfillment? Why?

The most important part of my day is the morning shower. It is the only part of my day that modern technology is not on my person, a uniquely freeing set of minutes where even the loudest buzzes are drowned out by rushing water.

I use my time in the shower tactfully. I brush my teeth as lukewarm drizzles become soothing steam, and I step into the tub. I begin by singing. I rehearse a capella arrangements and test country or Spanish covers of my favorite tunes. In the confines of the ceramic wall and flowing curtain, my reverberating echoes feel infinite, and only my own ears can hear my hymns.

It is also a time to practice presentations, review yesterday’s arguments, test the limits of my imagination. I allow my thoughts to flow naturally, even in circles if they need to. When my windpipe, skin, and mind are clean, the faucet can be shut off. Drying off, I am warmed up, with a clearer voice and mission for the day. As I leave the bathroom, I am my whole self.
