4/12 Reading Response to: "Do Artifacts Have Ethics?"

Like this reading notes, discourse around firearm ethics usually only revolves around one question: are guns inherently dangerous or are people inherently dangerous? While this individual thought can produce millions of genuine arguments and debates, it can create massive polarization, lack nuance, and avoid solving any problems. When the problem is widespread and deadly, it’s important we ask the right questions when we talk about it. “Do Artifacts Have Ethics?” raises 41 questions, each targeting a distinct way we interact with a technology, how it interacts with us, and how it interacts with its surroundings. This approach to product development and management should be universal, ensuring we understand the scope and use of a technology, whether it’s a pistol or a packaged mac and cheese.
