4/13 Writing Exercise- Prompt: What is a human-made creation that brings out the best in you? Why?

A piano brings out the best in me. This is not because I am especially amazing at the piano; I don’t really have music theory down despite my grandma’s attempts over the last decade or two. Rather, I have a personal connection to the piano. As I raise my hands up from my lap, my fingers feel a natural pull towards a certain set of keys. My first chord usually is different each time, but it typically is a 7th chord, which I like because it doesn’t feel major or minor in nature- it’s more of a purplish or greenish blend of emotions, a deeper, more nuanced timbre. From this first chord or set of chords I add features, tones, perhaps my voice or a percussive tap. The piano provides all of the tools to generate and bend noises, allowing me to press down an endless story, a collection of memories and imaginations, into the instrument. I can sense the vibrations ring off the strings in the piano’s cabinet, and I imagine if I were a bug I’d feel them pulse into the wooden floor too. If the piano is a bed of soil with 88 seeds, my fingers spray spritzes of water and my eyes shed the light of the sun.
