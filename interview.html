The Architecture Of Stress

A 20-minute conversation with a future Investment Banker on the visual friction between professional data and personal recovery.

Interviewer: Jeremy Cohn || Interviewee: Lucas Bernicker

View Full Transcript

01. Workplace

Emotions and Design: What kind of emotions are drawn out when you're scrolling through spreadsheets and data sheets for hours?

"I found that is certainly a large hurdle of the job... emotions of frustration, confusion, I think are things that come top of mind to me."

The "Flow State": Do feelings of frustration and confusion ever turn into direction when there is no other stimuli and you are looking at straight raw data?

"I do definitely achieve kind of that flow state... when you are just drilled down to the numbers... there does become a point where that's all you're focused focusing on, and it's easier to spot trends in the data."

Mental Exhaustion: Did you feel exhausted mentally at the end of a work day, and if so, what things did you do at work to take away from that?

"Information overload in my profession is massive, right? ... what I would is have screen down-time, where I'm either writing my thoughts on pencil and paper, or I'm just talking to someone else."

Visual Trust: What do websites do to make you feel like information is trustworthy and relevant?

"I want to see a very standardized, clean esthetic, kind of just information first, not a lot of filler."

Color Psychology: In finance, the color red is negative; when you see that color outside of work on your phone, does that trigger an emotional response?

"Red in markets is associated with loss... and I remember waking up a lot, and during the summer, checking markets, seeing a lot of red, and it just puts a feeling in your stomach that you don't want to have."

02. Leisure

Algorithm vs. Discovery: Do you prefer content being curated and "thrown at you," or do you like to seek out and organically discover information?

"I guess I do like when it's suggested to me, but not an aggressive manner, not like a pop up saying, "oh, you should watch this next.""

Interactivity vs. Static Screens: For reading-based sites, do you prefer a lot of movement and interactivity, or would you rather have a static screen to explore and scroll through?

"I like how substack is... when you're reading someone's substack, it kind of just is a wall of text, right? And there's not a lot of additional pop ups, movement, or animations... I'm looking for simplicity."

Managing High Stimulus: As someone with ADHD, what do you do to stop yourself from the over-consumption of high-stimulus information on your phone?

"Part of it's like just having internal limits with yourself, obviously... and then I think others like, if you know you're gonna kind of fall victim to it, my mind thing is like, let me at least curate the things that I'm going to be kind of rotting on to be more positive to my life."

03. Design Philosophy

Ideal Design: If you were to design a website of your own to feel calm and relaxing for the user, what aspects would you prioritize?

"I would say like number one would be very easy navigation, well designed user flows... Secondly... things should disappear into the background, or kind of move off screen when they're not needed... and this all ties into this idea of simplicity, clarity, and lack of confusion."
