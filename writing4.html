4/20 Writing Exercise- Prompt: When do you cherish the slow or hard way of doing something? Why?

There are certain aspects of the beginning of the pandemic that I really appreciate. While the world was shutting down and millions, including some of my family members, were getting sick and hospitalized, there seemed to be a unique chance for fresh beginnings.

With virtually no responsibilities outside of measly asynchronous class assignments, I had what felt like infinite time and freedom even in a locked down city. So, I tried out running, and I completely sucked. The first few attempts, I made it half a mile with my mom before cramping and sadly walking home. Day by day, I saw small improvements, accompanied by stretching YouTube videos and mileage trackers. By the end of the summer, I made my high school’s cross country team, kickstarting a passion for running that completely shaped my high school experience. I felt in shape, created incredible social connections, and built resilience.

Nowadays, my knees sometimes hurt when I run and it’s been well over a year since I’ve had a consistent weekly routine. I want to pull the discipline I had from that era and bring it into my current life. How can I get myself back into cardio shape, even if it is a painfully gradual process? How can I face adversity with strength and hope, knowing that day by day things can improve if I push myself? And how can I find liberty in moments of my life that feel compressed and stuck? My intense nostalgia for a complex and rebirthing period may actually help me strive for and achieve future success.
